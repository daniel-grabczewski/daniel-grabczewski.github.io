Identity, Values, Strengths

1. Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

At one point in time, I had done something bad. However, I could easily get away with it by blaming someone else, and everyone would believe me. However, I didn’t. I blamed myself and I convinced everyone that I had done the wrongdoing. And I was punished for it.

In this decision, I weighed up my values of honesty and integrity, which I believe are at the core of who I am. Because of that, I reflect positively on the decision I made, and I know I would make the same decision today.

2. Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

My parents had a very strict, old-fashioned culture where your emotions were secondary to everything else. Because of this, when I was feeling sad as a child, I received little to no support from my parents. Their answer was that it was my fault that I feel that way, and I need to “suck it up.”

Because of this lack of emotional support from my own parents, I had to become very self-reliant and independent when growing up. As a result, I never properly developed the ability to trust people and to show myself as vulnerable; I was afraid of being rejected like I was with my parents.

So, as a result, my identity is synonymous with being independent and self-driven; because this was the way I had to be in order to survive.

3. Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

Logical: Logic is essential for being able to write good clean code, and, more importantly, solve problems in general. Only by thinking logically can you create effective solutions.

Determined: When I start any kind project or learning journey, I will remain determined throughout its entire duration to succeed.

Open-minded: It will mean that I am open to new ideas and will not let my pride get in the way if someone tells me that I can do something differently/better. I will have a growth mindset.

4. Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

I have difficulties in participating in large group discussions. As a result, this could affect my ability to be heard when doing a collaborative project with other people, or asking questions in front of the rest of the class.

5. Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

I was once working in a group of three people on a creative project. However, me and another group member had very different creative direction at the time. To settle the difference, we asked the third group member on what creative direction was better. After that, we agreed to go with what the third group member said.

This was an effective way to move forward with a creative direction. However, it was not an effective way of making everyone feel included in the group. My creative direction was put forward. But the other group member felt down that their idea had been tossed to the side like it was worthless.

If I were to make this decision today, I would try to come to a compromise. We would all collaboratively discuss both creative directions and why, objectively, certain aspects of each idea work better than the other. We would then reach a logical conclusion where everyone is satisfied.
