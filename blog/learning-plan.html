Cultural Blog - Your Learning Plan (for bootcamp)

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

I want to become a remote web developer. I’m not looking to be remarkable, just to make a good living and to not be tied down to an office building.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

I think the strengths and limitations from my foundations learning plan are the same. A strength of mine I have discovered, however, is I have a knack for collecting my thoughts in an organised manner in order to solve a problem. Because of that, pseudocode is an excellent strategy for me that I will use for the rest of my career.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

I think my biggest challenge will be speaking in front of a group, and working in a group.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

The ability to participate in a large group discussion.

The ability of working with others without turning it into a competition, but a collaboration.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

I expect them to be there when I cannot solve a technical issue and I have done all I can and reached out to other students without success. I also expect them to be effective teachers.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp

I will seek help with the teachers when I cannot solve a problem myself, or with the help of another student. That way, I will never be stuck on anything for an extended period, so I can keep moving forward.

I will work productively by not distracting other students and also being upfront to any other students possibly distracting me. I will always act civil and never allow my emotions to make me act in a rash way.







Cultural Blog - Your Learning Plan (for foundations)

1. What is your long term goal or career pathway?

Become a Web Developer that works remotely.

2. A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning

My strengths are discipline. I am a logical thinker and I am determined to achieve any goal I set my mind to.

My limitations are the ability to participate in a large group discussion. Also, being overly-critical of myself (perfectionism), and being overly-competitive.

3. What skills (non-technical core/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

  • The ability to participate in a large group discussion.
  • The ability of working with others without turning it into a competition, but a collaboration.

4. A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme. This should include:

An explanation of how you will manage yourself to work productively and safely with other learners, Facilitators and industry/community representatives:

  • I am working with a 52/17 rule. It means that for every 52 minutes working, I take a 17 minute break. This helps me be productive throughout the day.
  • I am always polite and civil when communicating with anyone. I never let my emotions take control over my communication. So, if I’m feeling frustrated with the workload, I’m never going to bring it out on anyone. I will discuss the problem I am having in a calm and orderly manner.

What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you?

  • I will take a few minutes to step away from the action. I have learned a release method that helps me get rid of negative feelings that have built up in me. I’ve been using this method for a few weeks and it works very well for me.
  • If necessary, I can reach out to a fellow student or facilitator for help if the root of the issue is that I am stuck on something.

5. A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

When I am stuck and I need help, my first resort will be the internet. If the internet doesn’t solve my problem within 20 minutes, I will then seek help from a fellow student or facilitator on Discord.

6. A description of what you expect from the Facilitation team

I expect the facilitation team to be able to provide help when I am stuck with something. I hope they are able to explain things in a simple manner, without over-complicating their explanation. Also, they should not make me feel bad if I don’t understand something the first round by showing any kind of frustration or annoyance.

7. Any scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things. For example, you might have whānau commitments like school drop-offs. It's important to plan these in.

I have no other commitments during my study at Dev Academy.
