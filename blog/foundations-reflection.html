Foundations Reflection

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

I learned that I always believed that intelligence was somewhat fixed, until we were taught about neuroplasticity. And now I know that intelligence isn’t fixed at all.

I learned that I have good emotional intelligence after learning about what emotional intelligence exactly is. I also learned how important it is for me to keep myself as an emotionally intelligent person.

I learned that my words feel more valued when someone is listening to me attentively and that it makes me more confident.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

The role of values and empathy in learning and programming promote being able to work with others. If you’re not able to have these values, then you’ll find working with others to be very difficult for yourself, and on them.

The role of self-awareness in learning and programming is for you to be aware of when you are struggling and when you are succeeding, when you’re stressed and when you’re not. This way, you can reach out for help when you need it and not cave in on yourself.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

What surprised me about core learning was how useful it was to being a better programmer. At first, I had doubts whether or not core would be a waste of time. But once I began to see how it made me a better programmer by applying the things I learned, then I saw core as being very valuable.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

The most challenging aspects of core learning was in times where I had to question myself and my identity to figure out my own learning plan. This was difficult because it was something I had never really done. So reaching deep into my soul to find my identity was a bit daunting at first.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

I think Dev Academy spends a lot of time focusing on core skills because they recognise that it is one of the most important skills to have when applying for a web development job.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

I believe that learning core wasn’t a waste of time. If I had instead spent my time on practising programming, maybe I would be able to learn more syntax and understanding of how code works. However, I wouldn’t be a better programmer. Because a programmer needs to have a healthy mindset, or else they will eventually crash sometime in the future, or they’ll give up before they can succeed.
