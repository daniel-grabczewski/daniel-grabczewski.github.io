Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

Discuss how understanding the principles of Neuroplasticity benefits people.

By understanding the principles of neuroplasticity, people who are learning can believe that their intelligence isn’t ‘fixed’, but malleable. As a result, they are less likely to give up quickly when approached by a challenge because they know they aren’t ‘dumb’, they just have yet to make those neuron connections. In one word: optimism.

Discuss how you might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your benefit.

I can engage with the principle of neuroplasticity by knowing that when I come across a challenge, I will no longer view it as something that “stops” me, but something I have yet to accomplish.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity?

Daily deliberate practise on a particular activity can increase your neuroplasticity. This is because by constantly challenging your brain with a particular activity, the neurons connecting needed for those activity will grow stronger and stronger with each practise you make.

Link to a resource that you found particularly useful or engaging.

I found Carol Dweck’s TED talk on “growth mindset” to be very useful and engaging.




Discuss what is Growth mindset and why it is relevant.

Growth mindset is the mindset of being on a search for self-improvement. It means that you accept challenges. It means you don’t see failure as a way to stop yourself, but as a necessary step towards success.

In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you?

What surprised me is that just by knowing that intelligence isn’t something fixed, but malleable, that children can perform better. It made me see just how powerful knowing about neuroplasticity and growth mindset is. So once you begin to put these things into practise, you can be unstoppable and achieve anything you want.

How will you integrate growth mindset into your learning journey?

I will integrate growth mindset into my learning journey by always trying to welcome failure, rather than condemn it. I will try to accept that failure is a necessary step towards success.
